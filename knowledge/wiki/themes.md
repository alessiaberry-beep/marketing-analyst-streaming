# Paramount+ Strategy Wiki

## Overview

Paramount+ is the direct-to-consumer streaming service operated by Paramount Global (now Paramount Skydance following the merger). As of late 2025, the service reported approximately 78.9 million subscribers globally, with 35.8 million paying subscribers in the United States. The platform is available in approximately 45 markets worldwide and is positioned to merge with HBO Max following David Ellison's acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery, expected to close in Q3 2026.

---

## Content Themes

### Data-Driven Content Optimization

Paramount+ has shifted away from a volume-focused content strategy toward a more analytical, efficiency-driven approach. Key elements include:

- **Audience Segmentation**: The company uses internal data to identify "carefully-defined specific audience segments" and tailors content to "super serve" these groups
- **Franchise Leverage**: Increased emphasis on franchises and leveraging content across multiple platforms
- **Content Spend Optimization**: Following "unacceptable" profit drops, Paramount implemented $500M in cost cuts with a focus on producing "the right content for the right audience at the right time"
- **Content Writedowns**: Strategic removal of underperforming content from the platform

### Core Content Pillars

| Content Category | Key Properties | Strategic Role |
|------------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Sci-Fi/Fantasy Franchises** | *Star Trek: Strange New Worlds*, *The Legend of Aang: The Last Airbender* | Tentpole programming driving subscriber acquisition |
| **Reality/Competition** | *RuPaul's Drag Race All Stars* | Consistent engagement driver with loyal fanbases |
| **Live Sports** | UFC ($7.7B, 7-year deal), NFL, Grammys, CBS Sports | Differentiation and live appointment viewing |
| **Unscripted Development** | New leadership (ex-Netflix exec Damla Dogan) | Growth area with focus on development pipeline |

### Sports as Strategic Differentiator

The UFC rights deal ($7.7B over 7 years) represents Paramount's largest content acquisition under new ownership, signaling:

- Sports and entertainment synergy as a core thesis
- Live events as churn reduction mechanism
- Premium sports content as subscriber acquisition tool

## Marketing Themes

### Efficiency-Focused Marketing

Marketing has been identified alongside content as one of the "two largest expense categories" in the streaming P&L. The strategic approach includes:

- **Spend Optimization**: Reducing marketing costs while maintaining subscriber growth
- **Data-Driven Targeting**: Using analytics to identify and reach key audience segments more efficiently
- **Cross-Platform Leverage**: Maximizing content promotion across Paramount's portfolio (CBS, MTV, Comedy Central, etc.)

### Event-Based Marketing Moments

Paramount+ capitalizes on tentpole events for marketing visibility:

- **Awards Shows**: Grammy Awards broadcast creates dual TV/streaming promotional opportunity
- **Sports Events**: UFC fight nights and NFL coverage provide consistent marketing touchpoints
- **Franchise Premieres**: *Star Trek* and other franchise content drives earned media and fan engagement

### Bundle Strategy

Paramount+ participates in various bundle arrangements to reduce customer acquisition costs:

- Walmart+ bundle partnership
- Potential future bundling with HBO Max post-merger

## Audience Targeting Themes

### Primary Audience Segments

Based on content investments and stated strategy, key audience segments include:

| Segment | Content Drivers | Characteristics |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Sci-Fi/Fantasy Enthusiasts** | Star Trek franchise, Avatar/Airbender content | Highly engaged, franchise-loyal, convention-attending |
| **Reality TV Fans** | Drag Race franchise, unscripted development | Social media active, high engagement with cast/contestants |
| **Sports Viewers** | UFC, NFL, CBS Sports | Primarily male, appointment viewing, pay-per-view conversion potential |
| **Families** | Nickelodeon content, animated films | Household decision-makers, lower churn profiles |
| **News/Live TV Audiences** | CBS News, live events | Older demographic, cord-cutter/cord-never transition targets |

### Subscriber Overlap Insights

Antenna Research data (January 2026) reveals important targeting considerations:

- **21.1%** of Paramount+ subscribers also pay for HBO Max
- **~14%** of total subscriber pool between both services subscribes to both
- Significant de-duplication opportunity post-merger, but also risk of revenue consolidation

### Geographic Considerations

- **Limited Global Footprint**: ~45 markets vs. Netflix (190) and HBO Max (110+)
- **U.S.-Heavy Subscriber Base**: 35.8M of 78.9M total (45%) are U.S. subscribers
- **European Strategy**: Investment in SkyShowtime joint venture with Comcast ($1B)

## Paid Social Implications for Marketing Analysts

### Campaign Strategy Recommendations

#### 1. Audience Targeting Approach

```
Primary Targeting Dimensions:
├── Interest-Based
│   ├── Star Trek / Sci-Fi fandoms
│   ├── UFC / MMA enthusiasts  
│   ├── Reality competition viewers
│   └── Nickelodeon nostalgia (millennials with children)
├── Behavioral
│   ├── Streaming service stackers
│   ├── Cord-cutters in transition
│   └── Sports betting app users (UFC correlation)
└── Demographic
    ├── HH income for premium tier conversion
    └── Family status for content personalization
```

#### 2. Content-Driven Campaign Moments

| Timing | Content Hook | Paid Social Focus |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Premiere Windows** | Star Trek S4 (July), Drag Race All Stars (May) | Awareness + subscriber acquisition |
| **UFC Fight Weeks** | Weekly fight cards | Retargeting, urgency messaging |
| **Awards Season** | Grammys, other CBS broadcasts | Cross-platform awareness |
| **Competitive Releases** | Counter-programming vs. Netflix/Disney+ drops | Conquest campaigns |

#### 3. Creative Optimization

- **Video-First**: Leverage premium content clips for thumb-stopping creative
- **Franchise Iconography**: Star Trek, Drag Race, SpongeBob have high visual recognition
- **Live Sports Urgency**: Time-sensitive CTAs for UFC events
- **Social Proof**: Subscriber milestones, trending content callouts

#### 4. Platform-Specific Considerations

| Platform | Primary Use Case | Key Audience |
|----------|------------------|--------------|
| **Meta (FB/IG)** | Broad awareness, family content | 25-54, household decision-makers |
| **YouTube** | Trailer views, content discovery | Franchise fans, cord-cutters |
| **TikTok** | Cultural moments, Drag Race clips | Gen Z, reality TV enthusiasts |
| **X/Twitter** | Live sports conversation, real-time | UFC fans, news consumers |
| **Reddit** | Fandom communities (r/startrek, etc.) | Deep engagement, conversion |

#### 5. Measurement Framework

**Key Performance Indicators:**
- **Subscriber Acquisition Cost (SAC)**: Benchmark against industry (~$50-80 for streaming)
- **Trial-to-Paid Conversion Rate**: Critical for promotional campaigns
- **Content-Attributed Signups**: Which content drives acquisition
- **Churn Correlation**: Campaigns targeting high-churn-risk segments
- **ARPU Impact**: Premium tier vs. ad-supported tier acquisition mix

#### 6. Competitive Considerations

- **Netflix**: 325M+ subs globally—focus on differentiated content (sports, Star Trek)
- **HBO Max**: 27.1M U.S. subs—pre-merger, conquest opportunity; post-merger, integration
- **Disney+**: Family content overlap—emphasize Nickelodeon differentiation
- **Peacock**: NBC/Universal sports overlap—UFC exclusivity as differentiator

### Budget Allocation Considerations

Given Paramount's stated focus on marketing efficiency, analysts should:

1. **Prioritize High-Intent Channels**: Search, retargeting over broad awareness
2. **Leverage Owned Media**: Cross-promotion on CBS, MTV, Paramount Network
3. **Focus on Franchise Tentpoles**: Concentrate spend around major premieres
4. **Test Sports-Adjacent Targeting**: UFC provides unique audience access
5. **Monitor Overlap Metrics**: Avoid paying to acquire subscribers who would convert
