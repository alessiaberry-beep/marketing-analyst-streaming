# Paramount Aims to Keep Some Top Streaming Ads Out of Programmatic Fray

Source: https://variety.com/2025/tv/news/paramount-streaming-ads-fixed-placement-programmatic-1236540728/
Retrieved: 2026-04-28
Published: October 6, 2025

## Summary

Paramount, now under the aegis of Skydance Media, is unveiling a new ad format on Paramount+ that ensures sponsors who choose it get seen by broader audiences, even if every streaming subscriber is free to watch at times of their own choosing.

## New "Streaming Fixed Units" Ad Format

### How It Works
- Advertisers who buy these units get the same ad placement in premieres of new episodes of top shows
- Fixed placement (e.g., first spot in a commercial break)
- Guaranteed placement for the first seven days after episode debut

### Featured Shows
- "Tulsa King"
- "Landman"
- "Mayor of Kingstown"

## Strategic Rationale

### Premium Inventory Control
The offering may give Paramount more control over specific types of streaming inventory that would allow it to seek a premium, or higher-than-expected pricing, rather than letting that ad time be sold via programmatic means.

### Programmatic vs. Fixed
Programmatic buying allows advertisers to snap up inventory via algorithms that use specific audience and consumer criteria. In streaming, every program is watched by individual consumers at different times, in different geographic regions and with different interests.

Paramount's new units give advertisers a chance to boost their programmatic stock by having a defined presence when bigger audiences are likely to watch popular programs in broader fashion.

## Advertiser Categories

The new units have been purchased by:
- Financial-services companies
- Marketers of alcoholic beverages
- Consumer product giants
- Backers of A.I.
- App publishers
- Automobile marketers
- Casual-dining chains
- Western lifestyle brands (e.g., Boot Barn targeting Taylor Sheridan series viewers)
