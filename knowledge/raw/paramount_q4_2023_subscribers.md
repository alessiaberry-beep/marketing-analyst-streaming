# Paramount+ Hits 67.5M Subscribers As Streaming Loss Shrinks to $490M

Source: https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/paramount-streaming-loss-subs-1235838684/
Retrieved: 2026-04-28
Published: February 28, 2024

## Summary

Paramount Global hit 67.5 million Paramount+ streaming subscribers worldwide at the end of its fourth quarter 2023, a gain of 4.1 million from the previous financial quarter. The Hollywood conglomerate said it expected to deliver "significant total company earnings growth" in 2024 and reach profitability for Paramount+ domestically in 2025.

## Key Metrics

### Paramount+ Subscribers
- 67.5 million worldwide at end of Q4 2023
- Gained 4.1 million from previous quarter

### Streaming Loss
- $490 million loss (improved from $575 million YoY)
- Reduced full year DTC losses in 2023
- Hit peak streaming losses in 2022, a year ahead of schedule

### Q4 2023 Financial Results
- Net earnings: $514 million (vs. $21 million YoY)
- Revenue: $7.63 billion (down 12%)
- Adjusted EPS: 4 cents (vs. 8 cents YoY)

## Path to Profitability

On the analyst call, CEO Bob Bakish underlined increases in viewer engagement, reduced churn, and a subscription price increase as bringing profitability to Paramount+ by 2025.

CFO Naveen Chopra: "I do think sub growth in 2024 will be lower than 2023, though importantly I'd point out we do still expect very healthy Paramount+ revenue growth and, of course, revenue is the more important metric than subs."

## Division Performance

### Direct-to-Consumer
- Advertising revenue: rose 14% to $526 million (Paramount+ and Pluto TV growth)
- Subscription revenue: grew 43% to $1.33 billion

### TV Media
- Revenue dropped 12% to $5.16 billion
- Advertising revenue fell 15% to $2.28 billion
- Affiliate/subscription revenue dropped 1% to just over $2 billion

### Filmed Entertainment
- Revenue: $647 million (down 31% from $936 million)
- Sharply lower licensing revenue

## Cash Flow

Generated $558 million of net operating cash flow and $443 million of free cash flow during Q4.

## Strategic Commentary

### Bundling Strategy
Bakish discussed partnering with rivals to offer streaming bundles: "We already have substantial experience with the power of bundling and streaming. We have hard bundles internationally with people like Sky, Canal and others. They've been key to our market entry strategy. They're unquestionably additive to our Paramount+ sub base and economics. There's also things like Walmart+ in the U.S., which is another form of bundle."

### Sports Strategy
Regarding Disney/WBD/Fox sports streaming venture: "There's still a lot we don't know about this service, things like price, packaging, consumer appetite. And to the consumer point, for a true sports fan, this product only has a subset of sports. It's missing half the NFL, a lot of college, has virtually no soccer or golf."

Bakish noted for viewers that come to Paramount+ for sports, 90% of their engagement is with non-sports programming.

## M&A Context

Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway reported a one-third stake cut in Paramount. Market speculation emerged about David Ellison's Skydance Media and RedBird Capital potentially eyeing a takeover of Redstone's controlling stake.

Byron Allen also made a $14.3 billion offer to buy all outstanding Paramount shares.

Bakish on M&A: "At Paramount, we're always looking for ways to create shareholder value. And to be clear, that's for all shareholders. But I'm not going to get into commenting on any speculation or timeline."
