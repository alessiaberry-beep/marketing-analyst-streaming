# Paramount Global Adds 3.5 Million Streaming Subscribers in Q3 Bruised by TV, Movie Declines

Source: https://variety.com/2024/tv/news/paramount-global-3-5-million-streaming-subscribers-q3-bruised-tv-movie-declines-1236204651/
Retrieved: 2026-04-28
Published: November 8, 2024

## Summary

Paramount Global added 3.5 million new Paramount+ subscribers in its third quarter, but those gains failed to offset meaningful declines in its larger TV and film businesses.

## Key Metrics

### Q3 2024 Results
- Total revenue: fell 6% to $6.73 billion (from $7.13 billion YoY)
- Earnings: $1 million, or 0 cents per share (vs. $295 million, 43 cents/share YoY)
- Adjusted earnings: 49 cents per share

### Paramount+ Subscribers
- 72 million subscribers at end of September
- Added 3.5 million in the quarter

### One-Time Charges
- $104 million charge for FCC license impairment
- $321 million charge for severance (layoffs and CEO Bob Bakish exit)

## Division Performance

### TV Media
- Revenue fell 6% to $4.3 billion
- Affiliate/subscription fees fell 7% (subscriber declines, no pay-per-view boxing)
- Advertising revenue fell 2% (despite political ad upticks)

### Filmed Entertainment
- Revenue fell 34% to $590 million
- Theatrical revenue off 71% (timing and number of releases)

### Direct-to-Consumer
- Ad revenue rose 18%
- Subscription revenue increased 7%

## Industry Challenges

Like its rivals, Paramount is struggling to move forward in an era when more consumers have moved from linear TV to streaming. Despite a library of popular content, Paramount is saddled with entertainment-focused cable networks (MTV, TV Land) that have seen their pull on the viewing public diminish over time.

## Cost Cutting and Skydance Merger

The company has worked to cut $500 million from its operations as it awaits a merger with Skydance Media. Paramount said it had made 90% of the cuts to reach its budget goals, with the rest expected by year-end.

"Our DTC segment successfully delivered profitability for the second quarter in a row, improving by more than $1 billion over the past four quarters, and, across the company, we continue to successfully execute non-content cost reductions that will result in $500 million in annual run rate savings," said the company's three CEOs.

## Skydance Transaction

Expected to close in the first half of 2025. Executives at Skydance have articulated a plan to make further cuts once the deal is consummated.
