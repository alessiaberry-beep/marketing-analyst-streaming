# Paramount Posts $49M Streaming Profit Amid Gain of 3.5M Subs, Film Unit Swings Back to Black

Source: https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/paramount-global-q3-earnings-streaming-skydance-1236053997/
Retrieved: 2026-04-28
Published: November 8, 2024

## Summary

Paramount Global reported its third-quarter financial results — including a swing to a streaming profit from a year-ago loss — in the Hollywood conglomerate's second earnings report since its sale to Skydance Media was unveiled.

That marked the second quarter of streaming unit profitability in a row, though CFO Naveen Chopra during a morning analyst conference call forecast the timing of content and marketing expenditures would produce a loss for the streaming business during the current fourth quarter, even as domestic profitability for its Paramount+ streaming platform in 2025 was expected.

## Key Metrics

### Paramount+ Subscribers
- Added 3.5 million subscribers compared with Q2
- Ended September with around 72 million subscribers
- Growth stemmed from international hard bundle agreements and Charter Communications distribution deal

### Streaming Financial Performance
- Adjusted operating income (OIBDA): $49 million profit (vs. $238 million loss year-ago)
- DTC segment revenue: rose 10% to $1.86 billion
- Advertising revenue: up 18%
- Subscriber revenue: up 7%
- Paramount+ revenue: jumped 25%
- ARPU expansion: 11%

### One-Time Charges
- $104 million charge related to FCC licenses value
- $321 million severance charge related to layoffs and departure of former CEO Bob Bakish

## Content Drivers

"Sports, including the return of the NFL and UEFA, originals like Tulsa King, which saw the biggest global debut in platform history for season 2, and Mayor of Kingstown, as well as post-theatrical releases, such as A Quiet Place: Day One and IF, all drove acquisition in the quarter."

## Skydance Merger Status

The transactions with Skydance are expected close in the first half of 2025, co-CEO Chris McCarthy reiterated. Fellow co-CEO Brian Robbins added Paramount continued to work to transform its streaming businesses and streamline the studio to reduce costs.

## Filmed Entertainment

- Adjusted OIBDA: $3 million (vs. -$49 million year-ago)
- Key driver: expenses declined from $940 million to $587 million
- Revenue fell 34% to $590 million
- Theatrical revenue decreased 71%

## TV Media

- Adjusted OIBDA fell 19% to $936 million
- Revenue declined 6% to $4.3 billion
- Affiliate/subscription revenue down 7%
- Advertising revenue fell 2%

## Total Company Q3

- Total revenue: declined 6% to $6.73 billion
- Operating income: fell 46% to $337 million
- Adjusted OIBDA: rose 20% to $858 million

## Cost Savings

In August, Paramount emphasized its focus on a $500 million cost-savings plan and goal of reaching sustained profitability in streaming by 2025. The company said its cost-savings plan would include reducing its U.S.-based workforce by around 15 percent.
