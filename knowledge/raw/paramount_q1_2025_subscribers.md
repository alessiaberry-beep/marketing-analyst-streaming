# Paramount+ Hits 79 Million Subscribers as Streaming Loss Shrinks to $109 Million

Source: https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/business-news/paramount-subscribers-streaming-loss-1236211289/
Retrieved: 2026-04-28
Published: May 8, 2025

## Summary

Paramount Global unveiled its first-quarter 2025 financial results as controlling shareholder National Amusements, led by Shari Redstone, looks to close the sale of the Hollywood studio to David Ellison's Skydance.

## Key Metrics

### Paramount+ Subscribers
- 79 million worldwide (up 11% YoY)
- Up from 77.5 million at end of Q4 2024
- Added 1.5 million subscribers in Q1 2025 (vs. 5.6 million in Q4 2024)

### Streaming Financial Performance
- Streaming loss: $109 million (improved from $286 million loss in Q4 2024)
- DTC revenue: rose 9% to $2.04 billion
- Advertising revenue: fell 9% to $473 million
- Subscription revenue: rose 16% to $1.57 billion (driven by Pluto TV and Paramount+)

### Company-Wide Results
- Net profit: $152 million (vs. $554 million loss year-earlier)
- Revenue: $7.19 billion (down from $7.68 billion YoY)
- Beat Zacks consensus estimate of $7.1 billion

## Path to Profitability

Company execs reiterated the studio "remains on track" to reach domestic profitability with Paramount+ for full-year 2025.

## Hit Content
- Landman
- Yellowjackets
- Yellowstone

## Taylor Sheridan and 101 Studios

Despite prizing its exclusive overall deal with Taylor Sheridan (running to 2028), Paramount does not plan to acquire 101 Studios, the production banner behind the Yellowstone creator.

"As it relates to 101 (Studios), they're absolutely a preferred partner. We love working with them... But we do like the relationship we have," co-CEO McCarthy told analysts.

McCarthy added Paramount was keen to allow Sheridan to maintain his creative control: "He's a unique creative and we built a model that was uniquely around him, as opposed to forcing him through our structure."

## Division Performance

### TV Media
- Revenue fell 13% to $4.53 billion
- Advertising revenue fell 21% to $2.03 billion (vs. Q1 2024 with Super Bowl LVIII)
- Excluding Super Bowl, TV media advertising was flat

### Filmed Entertainment
- Revenue rose 4% to $627 million
- Lower theatrical revenue offset by higher licensing revenue
- Supported by Q4 2024 holdovers: Sonic the Hedgehog 3, Gladiator II
- Q1 2025 debut: Novocaine

## Cost Management

Co-CEO Chris McCarthy: "We have not seen a meaningful impact due to the dynamic macro-environment. That said, looking forward, given the uncertainty, we are prioritizing key investments, while taking incremental steps to streamline non-content expenses."

CFO Naveen Chopra noted "growing macro-economic uncertainty, particularly in advertising, has the potential to impact our results later this year."

Co-CEO Brian Robbins: Paramount Pictures has reduced average production costs for tentpole movies by 35% over the last two years to "drive profitability."

## Upcoming Release

Mission Impossible – The Final Reckoning set for global release in Q2 2025. The studio has been teasing the eighth Mission Impossible film from Skydance as the "final" entry in the franchise to boost audience interest.

Expected to be completed in the first half of 2025.
