# More Than a Quarter of HBO Max Subscribers in the U.S. Already Have Paramount+

Source: https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-news/hbo-max-paramount-plus-combination-how-many-subscribers-1236519619/
Retrieved: 2026-04-28
Published: March 2, 2026

## Summary

Paramount Skydance chief David Ellison announced that once his company also owns Warner Bros. Discovery, the media giant will have a bit more than 200 million direct-to-consumer subscribers. The combined subscriber base analysis reveals significant overlap between the two services.

## Combined Subscriber Count

### Total Subscribers (End of 2025)
- Paramount+: 78.9 million subs
- Warner Bros. Discovery: 131.6 million (including linear HBO and Discovery+)
- Combined total: 210.5 million subs (not counting BET+)

Ellison estimates the deal to close in Q3 2026.

## Subscriber Overlap Analysis

### U.S. Subscriber Data (Antenna Research, January 2026)
- Paramount+ paying subscribers in U.S.: 35.8 million
- HBO Max paying subscribers in U.S.: 27.1 million

Note: This does not include linear HBO subs or select bundles like Walmart+.

### Crossover Statistics
- 7.6 million U.S. subscribers have both HBO Max and Paramount+
- 27.9% of U.S. HBO Max subscribers already have Paramount+
- 21.1% of U.S. Paramount+ subs also pay for HBO Max
- Almost 14% of the total subscriber pool between HBO Max and Paramount+ subscribes to both services

## Competitive Context

- Netflix: surpassed 325 million subscribers as of Dec. 31
- Netflix is available in 190 regions
- HBO Max: available in 110+ markets
- Paramount+: available in about 45 markets

## Revenue Implications

A de-duplication of subscribers would not create an exact one-to-one consolidation of subscription revenue. The combined service will not cost what Paramount+ or HBO Max cost individually, nor will it likely cost what both services cost together. As ARPU (average revenue per user) rises, overall subscription revenue will follow.

## Plan to Combine Services

Ellison stated he will combine Paramount+ and HBO Max into one service.
