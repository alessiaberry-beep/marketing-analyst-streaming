# Paramount Revenue, Streaming Subscribers Inch Up In Q4, But Losses Widen On TV Slump

Source: https://deadline.com/2026/02/paramount-q4-2025-earnings-david-ellison-warner-bros-discovery-1236735953/
Retrieved: 2026-04-28
Published: February 25, 2026

## Summary

Paramount posted a 2% uptick in total revenue in the fourth quarter to hit $8.15 billion, but losses widened during the first full quarter since the Skydance merger.

## Key Financial Metrics

### Q4 2025 Results
- Total revenue: $8.15 billion (up 2% YoY)
- Diluted losses per share: 52 cents (vs. 31 cents loss in Q4 2024)

Note: The $8.4 billion merger of Paramount and Skydance closed in August 2025. Quarterly numbers were compared with prior year on a pro forma basis.

### Paramount+ Subscribers
- 78.9 million subscribers (up 4% YoY)
- Now excludes free trials (prior periods restated for comparison)

## Division Performance

### TV Media
- Revenue slipped 5%
- Advertising revenue down 10% (due to absence of political ad buying vs. 2024)

### Streaming/Direct-to-Consumer
- Revenue up 10%

## Warner Bros. Discovery Merger Negotiations

In Paramount's quarterly letter to shareholders, Ellison said he and his management team are "confident in our standalone strategy and growth trajectory for Paramount." At the same time, "we view WBD as an accelerant to achieving these goals more quickly."

Unlike Netflix Co-CEO Ted Sarandos, who has been actively campaigning for Netflix's proposed deal for WBD's studio-and-streaming division, Ellison & Co. have kept a low profile. Sarandos testified before a Senate committee, while Ellison agreed only to respond in writing to questions from lawmakers.

## Stock Performance

Paramount shares dipped 2% on the earnings report despite the good news on the WBD front. The stock has skidded 24% since the start of the year amid repeated efforts to gain traction with an initially hostile bid for WBD. Shares are essentially flat since the close of the Skydance deal last summer.
